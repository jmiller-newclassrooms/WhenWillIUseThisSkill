Math in Context: Subtract Integers

Use Case 1: Urban Exploration

Urban explorers investigate abandoned buildings and underground tunnels. They may need to subtract negative values representing the depth of underground passages to calculate their total length.

Use Case 2: Iceberg Research

Researchers studying icebergs need to calculate changes in iceberg depth below sea level. They might need to subtract integers to determine the iceberg's position in the water.

Use Case 3: Music Therapy

Music therapists use music to help patients with emotional and psychological issues. When analyzing mood changes in patients, they might need to subtract negative values representing a decrease in anxiety levels.

Use Case 4: Environmental Soundscaping

Soundscapers create natural sound environments for various purposes, such as stress reduction. They may need to subtract negative values representing sound intensity levels to calculate the overall sound intensity.

Use Case 5: Ghost Hunting

Paranormal investigators use specialized equipment to measure paranormal activity. They might need to subtract integers when analyzing changes in temperature to identify anomalies.
