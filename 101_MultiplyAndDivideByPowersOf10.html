Math in Context: Multiply & Divide by Powers of 10

Use Case 1: Astrobiology

When studying extremophiles (organisms that can live in extreme environments) on other planets, scientists may need to convert temperatures from Celsius to Kelvin (multiply by 1.0) or back to Celsius (divide by 1.0) to understand the habitability of those environments.

Use Case 2: Forensic Entomology

Forensic entomologists use the growth rate of insects on a corpse to estimate the time of death. They might need to multiply or divide by powers of 10 when converting between hours, days, and weeks.

Use Case 3: Cryptography

Cryptographers use large prime numbers to create secure encryption. They may need to multiply and divide these numbers by powers of 10 to manage the complexity of their algorithms.

Use Case 4: Ethnobotany

Ethnobotanists study traditional plant knowledge. They may need to convert measurements of medicinal plant compounds from grams to milligrams (multiply by 1,000) for precise analysis.

Use Case 5: Marine Archaeology

When exploring shipwrecks in deep-sea environments, marine archaeologists use remotely operated vehicles (ROVs) with cameras. They might need to convert depths from meters to kilometers (multiply by 0.001) to map the site accurately.
